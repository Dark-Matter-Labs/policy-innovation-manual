01 · The Support Model

Integrated, Light
& Deep

A tiered model that configures support to the readiness, capacity and challenge profile of each city or cluster. The three tiers are not stages every city passes through — they are distinct modes of support, deployed in response to what each situation requires.

The original task specification focused primarily on establishing and running dedicated policy settings — what became the deep and light tiers. The team made a deliberate decision to also resource integrated support, embedding policy awareness and diagnostic capability across the NZC city-facing team. Without this, the dedicated settings would have operated in a silo, disconnected from the city relationships and challenge diagnosis that make them effective.

SelectionHow we decide who gets what level of support

An initial scoping session is always offered — helping cities define and refine their challenge before committing to a deeper engagement. Selection is then shaped by content and contextual criteria:

Diversity & complementarity

Range of geography and emissions domains, and cases that complement rather than duplicate one another.

Readiness & timeliness

City readiness, motivation, and whether the moment is right — a live policy window or an emerging mandate.

Relationships & replicability

NZC's existing knowledge and relationships in that context, and whether learnings can transfer across the Mission City cohort.

Tier 01

Integrated support

Continuous · embedded · demand-building

Integrated support embeds policy innovation within existing NZC city support processes, treating it as one dimension of a broader integrated offer rather than a standalone function. It is the primary mechanism for building demand and the diagnostic pipeline from which deeper engagements emerge.

Intensity
Continuous, low-dedicated-resource
Primary purpose
Surfacing challenges & building the pipeline

Tier 02

Light support — Policy Lab Sprints

4–6 weeks · tied to a policy window

Light support provides focused, time-limited engagement tied to a defined policy window. The EU Policy Lab Sprint — four to six weeks, structured sessions, a clear output — is the primary example. It convenes city, national and EU actors around a specific legislative moment to produce grounded city input that would otherwise be absent from EU policy processes.

Tier 03

Deep support

6–12 months · complex systemic challenge

Deep support involves sustained, multi-phase engagement — typically six to twelve months — on a complex systemic challenge. It produces a portfolio of outputs including diagnostic maps, intervention portfolios, policy briefs, and in some cases pilot activity. Deep cases should be intentionally designed with clear pathways for spreading learning to other cities; the investment is only justified if the insights are transferable.

Resourcing reality Demand for policy innovation support consistently exceeds available resources at all three tiers. To produce deeper impact, this work requires sustained resource and investment — single sessions cannot cause transformative change. The strategic question for the next phase is not simply one of scale, but of integration: how can policy perspectives be brought more effectively into other workstreams and funded city programmes so that resources compound rather than stretch thin?
