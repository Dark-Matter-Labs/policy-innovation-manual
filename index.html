Policy Innovation

Cities are not short of ambition. What Mission implementation requires is the harder work of navigating, aligning, and reshaping policy across every level of governance. This is a working record of how that work was done — the model, the methods, and the practice behind three years of it.

Task 6.5 exists to unlock action. It is an enabling and brokering function: getting the right people in the room over a sustained period, helping cities see what is structurally holding them back, and taking pragmatic steps toward change that would otherwise not happen.

Policy and regulation are simultaneously overtalked and underused — frequently cited as barriers, but rarely addressed directly. Addressing them means thinking specifically while thinking systemically: getting precise about where a decision is stuck, who holds power over it, and what would need to shift at that point. Part of the value of this work is diagnostic — distinguishing what is genuinely a policy challenge from what is instead organisational, cultural, or financial, so that effort is directed where policy instruments can actually make a difference.

The shape of the workThree interconnected tracks

Over SGA1 the work developed along three tracks. The first two exist to serve the third — without them, the dedicated settings would have operated in a silo, disconnected from the city relationships and challenge diagnosis that make them effective.

01 · Shared understanding

Building the language

Shared vocabulary and diagnostic capability across the NZC consortium, so that policy innovation settings are built on challenges that have actually been identified.

02 · Embedded support

Into existing channels

Policy support woven through the wider NZC offer — CCC reviews, the learning programme, one-to-one city accompaniment — building demand and the diagnostic pipeline.

03 · Dedicated settings

Where innovation happens

Studios, labs, sprints and sandboxes: focused settings where targeted policy innovation actually takes place, anchored in challenges cities are already invested in.

How to read this kitThe two-axis navigator

The work can be read two ways. By support level — how intensive and sustained the engagement is (Integrated, Light, Deep). Or by method — the operational format used (Studio, Lab, Sprint, Sandbox). The same practice sits at the intersection of both. Toggle between the two lenses.

Regulatory Sandbox Deep · national approval

A controlled space to test new regulatory approaches in real conditions before formal adoption. The most resource-intensive and politically complex format — it cannot be advanced by NZC or city teams alone.
