01 · Policy Innovation

Mission-Driven
Policy Innovation

Policy and regulation are simultaneously overtalked and underused — frequently cited as barriers, but rarely addressed directly. Addressing them means thinking specifically while thinking systemically: getting precise about where a decision is stuck, who holds power over it, and what would need to shift at that point.

Part of the value of this work is diagnostic — distinguishing what is genuinely a policy challenge from what is instead organisational, cultural, or financial, so that effort is directed where policy instruments can actually make a difference.

Engaging in policy innovation is a call to unlock action. It has an enabling and brokering function: getting the right people in the room over a sustained period, helping cities see what is structurally holding them back, and taking pragmatic steps toward change that would otherwise not happen.

The shape of the workInterconnected tracks

The Mission-Driven Policy Innovation work within NetZeroCities developed along three tracks. The first two existed to serve the third — without them, the dedicated settings would have operated in a silo, disconnected from the city relationships and challenge diagnosis that make them effective.

01 · Shared understanding

Building the language

A shared vocabulary and diagnostic capability across the NZC consortium, so that policy innovation settings are built on challenges that have actually been identified.

02 · Embedded support

Connecting into existing channels

Policy support woven through the wider NZC offer — City Climate Contract reviews, the learning programme, one-to-one city accompaniment — building demand and the diagnostic pipeline.

03 · Dedicated settings

Where innovation happens

Studios, labs, sprints and sandboxes: focused settings where targeted policy innovation actually takes place, anchored in challenges cities are already invested in.

Insight Embedded support does not map to a single method — it is the connective layer that surfaces challenges and builds the pipeline through City Climate Contract reviews, the learning programme, and one-to-one accompaniment. See the Support Model and the integrated portfolio.

Policy innovation in actionThree learnings

NetZeroCities' Mission-Driven Policy Innovation work surfaced three consistent requirements across the portfolio.

Learning 01

Grounded

Policy innovation works best when anchored in challenges cities are already invested in — but those challenges are often structural and need to be connected across governance levels before cities can fully see their relevance and act on them.

Learning 02

Collaborative

Traction depends on partners who are genuine co-producers and brokers — bringing mandate, local knowledge, and relational access that no single actor can effectively replicate alone.

Learning 03

Sustained

To produce transformative rather than incremental change, this work requires dedicated resources and sustained engagement. Single sessions cannot shift governance.
